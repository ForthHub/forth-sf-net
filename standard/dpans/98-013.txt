Subject: Withdrawal of obsolescent features

Submitted by:  Greg Bailey, Athena Programming

_____________________________P R O P O S A L ___________________________


Modify the Standard as follows:

   Remove the following obsolescent words, and all references
   thereto, from the Standard:

      #TIB   CONVERT   EXPECT   FORGET   QUERY   SPAN   TIB

Justification for consideration of proposal:

   Agenda item a:  Implements intent of TC (as expressed in 1.4.2.)
   Agenda item b:  Resolves an issue raised in Request For
                Interpretation Q0010.

Rationale:

   These words were marked as obsolescent in the first edition of the
   Standard for the reasons that their functions were widely
   conflicted in existing implementations, or that their functions
   were not reconcilable with the architecture of the Standard.

   At the time, the TC intended to withdraw these words in the future
   barring compelling evidence that this would cause problems.  Fair
   warning of this intention was published in section 1.4.2.

   Since X3.215-1994 was published, the TC has received no messages
   arguing for reinstatement of any of these words nor arguing against
   their withdrawal.  Indeed, the only discourse involving any of them
   has occurred in the form of a Request For Interpretation (Q/A0010)
   from an implementor who was uncomfortable with the TC's decision
   against prescribing the implementation details of QUERY.  This issue
   will become moot when QUERY has been withdrawn.

   Since conditions have developed as anticipated by the TC, it is
   therefore proposed that the TC act as it intended by withdrawing
   these seven features.

Michael Gassanenko:
   I vote against withdrawal of TIB and #TIB .  IMHO Forth
   needs write access to input source parameters. (The
   motivation is that target compilers need it.) TIB and
   #TIB do not adequately solve this problem, but their
   existence suggests an "almost portable" solution.
   If we withdraw TIB and #TIB , and do not introduce some
   other means to redirect the input source without starting
   interpretation, the situation becomes worse.
   OTOH, if there was e.g. SOURCE! ( addr len -- ), I would
   vote for removal of all obsolete words.
